About Boody

Boody was born in Sydney, Australia with the goal of designing simple, comfortable and attractive apparel using Earth-friendly materials. Boody’s founders had been searching for comfortable underwear and bodywear that also satisfied their personal pursuit of eco-friendly fabrics and processes. Then they discovered the power of bamboo textiles.

That’s why Boody is sourced from the finest quality yarn spun from bamboo, one of the fastest growing plants on earth. Boody’s bamboo is grown in regenerating forests and requires far less water than traditional cotton. The viscose is blended with a touch of nylon and elastane to create a fabric that’s superbly soft, stretchy and wrinkle-free. Boody is thermo-regulating, breathable and eco-friendly; the perfect fit for you and the planet.

Now headquartered in sunny San Diego, Boody is sold through retail partners like Pharmaca who are committed to social responsibility and selling eco-friendly goods. Boody ensures that every product created is good for both you and the earth. This collection of everyday essentials is stylish, soft, individualistic and, most of all, friendly to the world around us.

Boody Product Information

Ecocert: Boody is made from bamboo that is grown organically using no fertilizers or pesticides, as rated by the largest organic certification organization in the world.

OEKO-TEX Standard 100: Wear Boody textiles with confidence, knowing that the yarn contains no trace chemicals that pose health threats.

OCIA/IFOAM & USDA National Organic: Bamboo is grown in accordance with international organic standards. Each bamboo stalk is of 100% natural growth and free of any chemical pesticides.

WRAP (Worldwide Responsible Accredited Production): Boody takes labor and human rights seriously. Their clothes come from certified factories that adhere to no child labor, no exploitation, exceed minimum wages, and regulate work hours and sick/holiday pay.

Earth Friendly Packaging: Boody clothes are packed in boxes printed on recycled paper with natural, vegetable-based dyes.
