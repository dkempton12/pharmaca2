Why Human Studies?

“Currently, nine out of ten experimental drugs fail in clinical studies because we cannot accurately predict how they will behave in people based on laboratory and animal studies.”
—Mike Leavitt, U.S. Secretary of Health & Human Services

While test tube and animal studies may identify promising ingredients worthy of further study, human clinical studies are widely considered the gold standard of scientific research. Why? It’s simply not possible to make foolproof predictions about how substances will function in the human body from test tube or animal studies alone.

Humans differ from animals in anatomy, physiology and metabolism. Consider these examples:

  • Chocolate is delicious to you, but poisonous to dogs.
  • Tylenol is great for relieving your headache, but it’s toxic to cats.
  • Penicillin will help clear up your ear infection, but it will kill a guinea pig.

When you choose Quality of Life, you can trust your supplements to work, because 100 percent of the ingredients in every product they manufacture are backed by human clinical studies.

Why Do Dosages Matter?

Everyone knows blueberries are a healthy food. Multiple human clinical studies, using about 1 cup per day (about 200 berries), have proven this fact. But what if, in the hopes of getting healthier, you decided to eat a single blueberry a day? Common sense says that eating just one berry daily is unlikely to “keep the doctor away.”

This is consistent with an accepted principle of both conventional and integrative medicine: Dosages matter. Many natural compounds have been shown to be effective at specific threshold doses, yet have little or no impact at lower ones. Just because eating a daily cup of blueberries is beneficial does not mean that eating one berry a day will have any effect. Unfortunately, it is common practice in the nutritional supplement industry to use dosages of ingredients that are lower than those supported by clinical research.

When you choose Quality of Life, you can trust your supplements to work, because 100 percent of the ingredients in every product they manufacture contain dosages that match the clinical research.
